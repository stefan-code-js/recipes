CHOCOLATE
Active time
25 minutes
Equipment
Instant-read thermometerIngredientsBittersweet, milk, or white 
couverture chocolateTempering Chocolate, Water Bath Method
Chop the chocolate and place in a bowl over a saucepan  
of barely simmering water. Stir until melted, 122° F (50°C)  
for bittersweet chocolate, and 113° F (45°C) for milk and white.
When the chocolate has melted, stand the bowl in a larger bowl 
filled with ice cubes and water. Stir to lower the temperature  
of the chocolate.Cool bittersweet chocolate to 82° F–84° F (28°C–29° C), milk  
to 81°F–82° F (27°C–28° C), and white to 79° F–81°F (26°C–27° C). 
Put the bowl back over the pan and raise the temperature  
to 88°F (31°C) for bittersweet, 84° F (29°C) for milk,  
and 82°F (28°C) for white.1 •
