As elemental as its name, Flesh & Bone 
is what charcuterie looks like at chef Ollie Dabbous's 
acclaimed London restaurant Hide. The plate is at 
once stark and primal, the disembodied feathers 
and cured meat suggesting some prior violence 
that has been distilled into a savage elegance. Its 
preparation simply involves wrapping skewers with 
meat and setting them against a backdrop that 
brings to mind a winter hunting scene. There's 
something a bit Game of Thrones about it; it's a 
tableau where animalistic drama plays out before
