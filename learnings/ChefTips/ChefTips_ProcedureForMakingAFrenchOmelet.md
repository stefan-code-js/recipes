PROCEDURE for Making a French Omelet
See Figure 25.6 for illustration of technique.
1. Collect all equipment and ingredients.
2. Beat 2 or 3 eggs in a small bowl just until well mixed. Do not 
whip until frothy. Season with salt and pepper.
If desired, 1 tablespoon (15 mL) water may be added to 
make the omelet lighter.
For extended service, beat a large quantity of eggs. 
Measure each portion with a ladle.
3. Place an omelet pan over moderately high heat.
4. When the pan is hot, add about 1 tablespoon (15 mL) clari-fied butter and swirl it around to coat the inside of the pan. Give it a second to get hot.
Raw butter may be used, but great care is necessary to 
keep it from burning. Milk solids in the butter will brown, detracting from the appearance of the finished omelet.
5. Add the eggs to the pan. They should begin to coagulate around the edges and on the bottom in a few seconds.
