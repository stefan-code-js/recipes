Simmering Methods
the most common method for cooking rice and other whole grains is to place the washed grain in a 
heavy pot with the right amount of water or other liquid to hydrate it, bring it to a simmer, cover, and 
cook slowly until all the water is absorbed. the exact amount of liquid needed varies considerably, 
depending on these factors:
1. the type of grain, its age, and its moisture content
