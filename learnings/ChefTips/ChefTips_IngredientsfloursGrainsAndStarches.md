WHAT IS GLUTEN?
Gluten is formed by two proteins, glutenin and gliadin, which occur naturally in certain grains, including wheat, rye, barley, spelt, and oats. When liquid is added to flour, these two proteins bind together to make gluten, which plays a crucial role in the leavening and texture of baked goods. Kneading or mixing develops the web-like gluten network, which is particularly important for yeasted bread and brioche-type doughs. Gluten gives such doughs elasticity and traps gas as the dough bakes, helping it rise.
