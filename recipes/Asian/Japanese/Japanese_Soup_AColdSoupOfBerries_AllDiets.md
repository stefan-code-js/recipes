## Ingredients

- 250 g yellow beets (washed, skin on), halved
- 300 g quartered Japanese quince (with seeds)
- 300 g halved red ooseberries 100 linonberries
- 500 g topped and seeded rose hips
- 300 g Lacto Mirabelle Plums*
- 300 g Barley Koji* , in chunks
- 130 g Black Currant Wood Oil*
- Twenty g Mori ta Chile Oil*
- Ten g anise hyssop leaves
- Six g oreano leaves
- Six g rouhly picked lemon thyme leaves
- Seven g yarrow
- Twenty g frozen ramson leaves
- 125 g Cooked Yellow Beets
- TOMATO -SAFFRON WATER
- Ice-Clarified Tomato Water*
- 180 g Berry Soup Base
- Thirty g Smoked Koji Oil*

## Method

1. COOKED YELLOW BEETS
2. Sauté the beets in a bit of grapeseed oil to color them slightly, then cover the pan with
3. aluminum foil and transfer it to the oven set to 200°C (100% fan) for 1 hour to cook them through. Once cooked, remove them from the oven, allow them to cool, and set aside.

5. Place all the ingredients into a bread mixer setup with a paddle attachment. Mix on medium speed for 5 minutes, then transfer the mixture to the fridge overnight to infuse. Once infused,centrifuge the mixture at full speed for 15 minutes to separate the j uice. Strain the centrifuged
6. mixture through a fine -mesh nylon sieve and reserve. Discard the solids.
7. Cold -infuse the tomato water with 0.1% saffron by weight in a sealed container in the fridge
8. overnight. Strain the liquid through a fine -mesh nylon sieve the following day and reserve.
