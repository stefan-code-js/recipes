## Method

1. 159When making the brioche dough, remove 2 pounds of the dough from the mixing
2. bowl prior to bulk fermentation.
3. A day ahead, place the currants in a small bowl and pour in the liquor. Let
4. soak overnight. Drain, reserving the soaking liquor.
5. In a wide bowl, stir together the soaked currants, apricots, pistachios,
6. cardamom, and orange blossom water. Add to the brioche dough and, using your
7. hands, mix to combine.
8. Let rise at a warm room temperature (about 80°F) for about 2 hours. Give
9. the dough one turn per hour, following step 5 on page 54. Turn the dough out
10. onto a work surface and shape into a round. Brush a kugelhopf mold with some of
11. the melted butter and place an almond in each top crease around the crown. Make
12. a hole in the center of the dough and place the dough, seam-side up, in the mold.
13. Let rise at a warm room temperature (about 80°F) for 1 ½ to 2 hours.
14. Preheat the oven to 425°F. Bake the kugelhopf until golden brown, 35 to
15. minutes. Unmold onto a wire rack and brush with melted butter while warm.
16. Dust with some of the confectioner’s sugar and brush with the reserved soaking
17. liquor. If serving the bread a day or two later, wrap the cooled kugelhopf and
18. store at room temperature. Dust with confectioner’s sugar again before serving.
