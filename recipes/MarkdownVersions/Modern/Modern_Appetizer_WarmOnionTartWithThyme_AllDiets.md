# WARM ONION TART WITH THYME

**Cuisine Type:** Modern
**Course:** Appetizers
**Dietary:** AllDiets
**Season:** AllSeasons

**Prep Time:** None
**Cook Time:** None
**Total Time:** None
**Servings:** Six Servings
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

## Ingredients

- One Spanish onion Spanish onion
- One tablespoon unsalted butter
- One tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
- One sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed according to package directions sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed according to package directions

---

## Method

1. 1.
2. Peel and cut the onion in half through the root. Trim the ends and then julienne the onion lengthwise.
3. 2.
4. Melt the butter in a small sauté pan over medium heat. Add the onion and thyme and cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 to 15 minutes or until the onion is a deep golden brown. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Keep warm until ready to serve.
5. 3.
6. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 400°F.
7. 4.
8. Unfold the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured work surface and turn over to prevent the creases from splitting. Pat down gently to flatten the sheet.
9. 5.
10. Using a 1-inch round fluted cookie cutter, stamp out 6 rounds and transfer to an ungreased baking sheet. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes or until puffed and golden brown.
11. 6.
12. Transfer the rounds to a wire rack to cool. When cool enough to handle, use a serrated knife to split each round in half horizontally.
13. 7.
14. Assemble the tarts on small plates by spooning a generous amount of the warm onion mixture onto each of the bottom halves. Replace the tops and serve immediately on small plates.
