## Ingredients

- Twenty-Five g spelt
- Twenty-Five g emmer
- Fifty g konini wheat
- Fifty g mustard seeds
- Ten g Cucumber Juice Reduction*
- Twenty g Peaso Water Reduction*
- Thirty-Five g butter, at room temperature
- Fifteen g Hazelnut Oil*
- Two g lemon t hyme
- Fifteen g Hazelnut and Lobster Garum Oil*
- Ten g Lemon Thyme Oil*
- Ten g liquid from Salted Green Gooseberry Capers*
- Three g Roasted Kelp Salt*
- SALTED FRESH HAZELNUTS
- One kg fresh hazelnuts

## Method

1. g konini wheat
2. In small saucepots, cook all the grains separately in water by bringing them up from cold. Simmer the grains until al dente, drain them, and dry them on a towel. Once dry, roughly chop
3. the emmer and spelt separa tely, leaving the mustard seeds and konini whole. Combine
4. everything in the stated amounts: ½ part chopped spelt, ½ part chopped emmer, 1 part whole
5. konini, and 1 part whole mustard seeds.
6. In a large bowl using a whisk, combine all the ingredients together thoroughly without aeratingthe mixture. Reserve in a covered container in the fridge.
7. g Roasted Kelp Salt*
8. Pound the kelp salt with a mortar and pestle, then add the lemon thyme and pound until wellmixed. Muddle in the liquids until a smooth paste forms and reserve.
9. Remove the crown of each hazelnut but leave it in its shell. Place the nuts into three times their weight in salt brine in a vacuum bag and seal the bag on maximum. Store the salted nuts in the fridge until needed. When needed, remove the nuts from the sal t brine and discard the brine.
