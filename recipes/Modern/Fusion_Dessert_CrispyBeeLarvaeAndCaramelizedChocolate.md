# CRISPY BEE LARVAE AND CARAMELIZED CHOCOLATE

**Cuisine Type:** Fusion
**Course:** Desserts
**Dietary:** AllDiets
**Season:** AllSeasons

**Prep Time:** None
**Cook Time:** None
**Total Time:** None
**Servings:** Four Servings
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

## Ingredients

- 250 g bee larvae
- 500 g butter
- One L filtered water
- 250 g beeswax
- One kg sugar
- 350 g plus 150 blond milk chocolate
- One Hundred g cocoa butter

---

## Method

1. Transfer the bee larvae to a blast freezer set to - 36°C. In a rondeau on the stove, add half the
2. butter to the pot, and heat it till foaming. Toss in all the bee larvae to start roasting it. Keep
3. roasting the larvae while managing the heat by adding the remaining butter to keep the fat foaming continuously. Fold the larvae over in the with a rubber spatula pan continuously whileroasting. Once they’re crispy, strain the bee larvae out of the pan through a conical strainer andreserve the butter for the sea buckthorn birthday cake. Dry the larvae off on a paper towel tosoak up any excess oil. Transfer them to a 50°C dehydrator to dry out even further.
4. Sonicate the beeswax into the water at 30% amplitude until the mixture reaches 60°C. Once sonicated, strain the water through a fine -mesh nylon sieve and transfer it to a pot with the
5. sugar. Bring to a boil and cook until the mixture reaches a sugar content of 72 ºBx. Remove the
6. syrup from the stove, allow it to cool, and reserve.
7. place it into a water bath set to 43°C overnight. In the morning, pour the melted chocolate into
8. a metal mixing bowl and seed the chocolate bit by bit with the remaining 150 g chocolate. Use
9. an immersion blende r to blend in the seeding chocolate until the mixture cools down to 26°C.
10. Once it’s at 26°C, bring the mixture back up to 28°C -your working temperature -and hold it
