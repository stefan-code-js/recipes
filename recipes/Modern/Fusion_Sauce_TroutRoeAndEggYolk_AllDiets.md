# TROUT ROE AND EGG YOLK

**Cuisine Type:** Fusion
**Course:** Sauces
**Dietary:** AllDiets
**Season:** AllSeasons

**Prep Time:** None
**Cook Time:** None
**Total Time:** None
**Servings:** Four Servings
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

## Ingredients

- TROUT ROE AND EGG YOLK
- Thirty g Peaso*
- Five g Roasted Kelp Salt*
- Two mL Cold -Infused Dashi*
- Four g Peaso Water Reduction*
- 500 mL Lacto Koji Water* (made with Barley Koji*)
- 500 g peeled Skaen shrimp
- 250 g Shrimp and Koji Reduction
- EGG YOLK SAUCE
- Six fresh hen’s eggs

---

## Method

1. Blend and pass the peaso and cook it in a nonstick pan for 10 minutes to intensify the flavor.
2. Remove from the heat and transfer it to a bowl. Add all the remaining ingredients and blend to
3. incorporate. Reserve in a piping bag.
4. Blitz the two ingredients together in the Thermomix at max speed for 15 seconds, then reducethe speed to 6 and turn on the heat to 60°C. Blend and cook the shrimp for 15 minutes. Once finished, transfer the contents of the Thermomix directly to a vacuum bag submerged in ice tocool it down rapidly. Once cold, seal the bag, being careful it doesn’t overflow. Place the bag into a pot of boiling water and boil for 1 hour. Strain the mixture through a fine -mesh nylon
5. sieve and allow the contents to hang for 30 minutes for maximum extraction. Reduce th e
6. resulting liquid gently in a pot on the stove to a sugar content of 50 ºBx. Reserve vacuum sealedin the freezer until needed.
7. Mix the two liquids together thorough ly. Reserve.
8. Cook the eggs in a water bath set to 68°C for 25 minutes, then shock them in ice water. Crack the eggs open to harvest the yolks, gently washing them in a bit of cold water to clean off the white. Place 3 of the egg yolks into the shrimp, koji, kelp cure and cure them at room temperature for 2½ to 3 hours (depending on size). Place the other 3 eggs yolks in between 2
9. sheets of kelp and cure them at room temperature for 1½ hours. After 1½ hours, flip the kelp
10. around (so that the fresh side of the seaweed is now in contact with the eggs) and continue to cure them for another 1½ hours. Afterward, remove all the egg yolks from their respective cures
