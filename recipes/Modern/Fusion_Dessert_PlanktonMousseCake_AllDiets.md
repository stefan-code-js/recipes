## Ingredients

- BLACK CURRANT WOOD OIL SPONGE
- 560 g suar
- Six fresh hen’s eggs
- Two egg yolks
- 336 g Black Currant Wood Oil*
- 168 mL lemon juice
- 290 g tipo “00” flour
- Twenty-Seven g bakin powder
- Ten Danish Tophit plums
- Eighty g dried Danish Tophit Plums
- Forty g Aronia Kelp*
- Fifteen g Black Currant Leaf Oil*
- Ten g black currant shoots
- Ten mL plum aquavit
- MILK CRUMB
- 500 g milk powder

## Method

1. Whip the eggs with the sugar in a stand mixer to soft peaks. Then emulsify the oil in by drizzling
2. it in slowly. Follow with the lemon juice. Stop the mixer, remove the bowl, and sift in the flou r
3. nd baking powder. Return the bowl to the mixer, reduce the mixer’s speed, and turn it on to
4. incorporate the dry ingredients until everything is homogenized, then stop the mixer.
5. Transfer the mixture to two full- size gastro trays, lightly oiled and lined with a sheet of
6. parchment each. Bake the sponge in a preheated combi oven at 160°C (50% fan) for 15 to 20 minutes. Once cooked, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool in its pan.
7. Place the plums on perforated dehydrator mats and place them in a dehydrator set to 50°C todry for 1 hour. After an hour, the skin will ha ve loosened slightly. Peel the skin off with a turning
8. knife and make a small incision to remove the stone as well. Return the fruits to the dehydrato
9. nd continue drying them until they take the texture of a dried apricot. Reserve them vacuum
10. sealed in t he freezer until needed.
11. Chop the dried plum flesh with the aronia kelp extremely fine with a sharp chef’s knife. Crushthe black currant shoot well with a mortar and pestle. Mix the mince with the oil and black currant shoots and then season it to taste with the plum aquavit.
