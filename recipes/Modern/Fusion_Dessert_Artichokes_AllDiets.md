## Method

1. Steam the first mixture at 90°C (195°F) for 40-42 minutes,
2. depending on the size of the artichokes and cool in ice water.
3. Steam the second mixture at 80°C (180°F) for 12 hours and then
4. strain the liquid. Reduce the liquid to half, to use later for diluting
5. the truffle puree. Cut the artichokes cooked in butter into 1-cm­
6. thick slices and trim them to approximately 1cm diameter. Prepare
7. Preheat the oven to 160°C (325°F). Toast the nuts on a tray in
8. the oven for 20 minutes. While still warm, blend to a fine puree.
9. Truffle slices and truffle sauce
10. finely slice the big truffles and punch out 10 pieces per person
11. in different sizes. Peel the remaining truffles and combine them,
12. Jerusalem artichoke liquid. Bring to the boil, blend to a smooth
13. sauce and season with white wine vinegar and salt.
14. Preheat the oven to 200°C ( 400°F). Toast the hay in the oven for
15. of salt, and vacuum-pack everything together. Leave to macerate
16. Warm the artichoke and brown butter mixture, and drain. Sprinkle
17. with salt and cover 4-5 of the artichoke slices with the hazelnut
18. puree. Arrange all the artichoke slices on a plate and cover with
19. truffle slices. Add a few dots of yoghurt, pour the warm sauce around
20. the other components and finally drizzle hay oil around the plate.
