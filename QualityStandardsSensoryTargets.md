# Quality Standards & Sensory Targets

## Seasoning & Acidity

- **Salinity**: Final salinity rarely above 0.8–1.0% in finished sauces; brines 2–3%.

- **Finishing Acids**: Verjus, champagne vinegar, green apple juice, preserved lemon oil.

- **Application**: Add acid at the end for brightness; recheck salt post-acid.

## Reductions & Viscosity

- **Nappé Definition**: Sauce coats spoon, clean line when finger drawn; typically 1.02–1.04 SG or 103–105 °C syrup point with glucose.

- **Clarity**: Reduce uncovered with wide surface; skim often; avoid vigorous boil after clarity achieved.

## Color & Chlorophyll

- **Greens**: Blanch in 2% salt water 60–90 s; ice hard; reheat with oil, not water.

- **Purées**: Keep purées > 70 °C only briefly; better to warm bowls and plate fast.

## Crunch Retention

- **Tuiles**: Store in hot box at 60–65 °C, desiccant nearby; plate seconds before service.

- **Plating**: Crisp + hot element must avoid steam-trap by raised plating or hidden spacers.

## Temperature Windows

- **Warm Sauces**: 65–70 °C

- **Cold Gels**: 6–10 °C

- **Custards**: 45–55 °C (for aroma bloom)

## Plating Logic

- **Arrangement**: One focal volume, one accent arc, one scatter. Negative space intentional.

- **Oil**: Oil as underline, not headline. ≤ 10–12 drops per plate unless it is the sauce.
