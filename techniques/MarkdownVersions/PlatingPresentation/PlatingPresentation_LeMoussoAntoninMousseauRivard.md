## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. 6 PETONCLE 
LE MOUSSO -ANTONIN MOUSSEAU-RIVARD 
To serve a single scallop at Montreal's Le Moussa, chef Antonin 
Mousseau-Rivard creates a tableau that lends the humble bivalve an element of 
mystery

2. To achieve such a haunting effect, the chef first puts a bit of oil at the 
bottom of the bowl and covers it with ice

3. The scallop is seared and placed on top, 
anointed with spicy XO sauce, and garnished with edible flowers called tagetes

4. Finally, liquid nitrogen is poured over the dish, which diffuses the smell of the oil 
and primes the diner's taste buds

5. The orange petals offer the dish a bit of playful 
color and warmth, bringing to mind the arrival of spring atop a chalky cliff
