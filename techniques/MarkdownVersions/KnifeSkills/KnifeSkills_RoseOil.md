## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Rose Oil*  
Pine Vinegar*  
Dashi Reduction* 
Just before service, chop the Madagascar peppercorns till they are a size that makes it easy to 
season the mushroom with them

2. Temper the sliced mushrooms 30 minu tes before the guests
arrive —but not too early otherwise the mushrooms will get spongy

3. Set a yakitori  barbecue to
medium -high heat with bintochan charcoal

4. Brush one of the cut surfaces of the mushroom 
completely with the glaze

5. Place the glazed face of t he mushroom directly on the barbecue

6. While the first side is cooking, brush the opposite side of the mushroom with the glaze

7. The cooking should take approximately 45 seconds to 1 minute per side, depending on the mushroom itself

8. The mushroom should be  approximately 30% undercooked when it comes off the barbecue

9. Once the mushroom has been cooked to this point, send the mushroom inside

10. Slice the mushroom into 2 or 3 pieces, depending on the size of the mushroom
