## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Cut into 2-mm slices with a truffle slicer 
into ice water and use the trimmings for another dish

2. Shell the 
walnuts with a nutcracker

3. Soak the nuts in warm water, peel off 
the brown skin and finely chop the nuts

4. Sauce 
Reduce the birch wine to one-third, whisk in the butter while 
the reduction is still warm and add the water

5. Dry the bleak roe 
on kitchen paper (paper towels)

6. Rye bread 
Grate the bread into crumbs, fry slowly in plenty of butter until 
golden, then leave to cool and drain on kitchen paper

7. Serving 
In the middle of each plate, lean chestnut slices up against each 
other to give height and volume

8. Avoid stacking and clumping, 
which would spoil the intended light appearance of the dish

9. Sprinkle the cress and the ryebread crumbs over the chestnuts
