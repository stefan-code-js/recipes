## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Barbecued King Crab 
King crab is cold-smoked until the shells are almond-scented

2. The body 
and legs are divided

3. The collars are mopped with a glaze of chicken 
wing garum, cucumber reduction, and smoked butter as they cook over 
hot charcoal until touched with color

4. The shell and meat are cut into 
portions, with the meat arranged neatly inside the clean body shell

5. The crab legs are hot-smoked over birch bark, bathed in smoked 
butter, and then charcoal-grilled

6. The meat is freed from the leg shell, 
then nestled back in place, this time brushed with a glaze of deeply 
reduced chicken stock, ryeso water, and mushroom-kelp broth

7. A tiny spoonful of ant paste, made with ants, berry spice, and black 
currant wood oil, and droplets of horseradish emulsion are spread onto 
the meat, and the top shell is set on top to form a lid to encase it all

8. The crab collar is dressed with a warm sauce of mushroom-kelp 
broth, truffle juice, brown butter, and black truffle

9. The crab leg joins 
the collar on the plate and a sea snail shell filled with more horseradish 
emulsion is served alongside
