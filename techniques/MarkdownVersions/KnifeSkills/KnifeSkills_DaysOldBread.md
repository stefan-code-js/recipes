## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. DAYS-OLD BREAD
273
Spoon or ladle more custard base into the center of each bread slice

2. If the 
liquid leaks out of the bread and onto the skillet, the bread slices are not quite 
sealed

3. Continue cooking for 1 minute, pressing the slices slightly to seal

4. When 
the slices are full of custard base, carefully transfer the skillet to the middle 
rack of the oven

5. Do not turn the toast

6. Bake the slices for 12 to 15 minutes and then gently shake the pan

7. If the custard 
base is still liquid, continue baking and check again

8. Depending on the thickness 
of the slices, the custard may take up to 20 minutes to cook all the way through

9. The French toast is done when the custard seems solid and each slice appears 
inflated, as the custard soufflés when fully cooked

10. Meanwhile, to cook the bacon, heat a skillet over medium heat
