## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Mix together the spices, herbs, and salt.

2. If the shrimp or scallops are wet, dry them with paper

3. toss the shrimp or scallops with the dry seasonings.

4. Sauté the onion and garlic in a little clarified butter until they are tender and only lightly browned. remove them from the pan and set them aside.

5. add a little more butter to the pan and sauté the  seafood just until it is cooked.

6. return the onion and garlic to the pan and toss

7. Following the procedure in Figure 20.5, cut twelve 4-oz (120 g) escalopes from the fillet.

8. heat a thin film of oil over high heat in as many sauté  pans as needed to hold the salmon.

9. Sauté the salmon escalopes about 1–2 minutes per

10. plate the salmon. Spoon 1½ fl oz (45 mL) of sauce  around each escalope.
