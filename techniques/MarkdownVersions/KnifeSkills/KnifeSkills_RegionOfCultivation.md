Seeds (which are technically fruits)

5. METHOD OF CULTIVATION 
The plant is cut at ground level when the seeds are mature and grey-brown in colour

6. COMMERCIAL PREPARATION 
The seeds are left to dry a few days, and then threshed, cleaned, and further dried

7. NON-CULINARY USES
Perfumery; in herbal medicine for water retention, arthritis, and gout; in Ayurvedic medicine as a nerve stimulant and tonic
