## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Collect all equipment and food supplies.

2. Cut or trim the meat as required. Dry it thoroughly. For stews,

3. Brown the meat thoroughly on all sides in a heavy pan with a small amount of fat, or in an oven.

4. Remove the meat from the pan and brown mirepoix in the fat left in the pan.

5. Return the meat to the pan and add the required amount of liquid.

6. Add a sachet or other seasonings and flavorings.

7. Bring the liquid to a simmer, cover the pot tightly, and simmer in the oven or on top of the range until the meat is tender.

8. Remove the meat from the pan and keep it warm.

9. Prepare a sauce or gravy from the braising liquid. This usually includes the following:

10. Combine the meat (sliced or whole) with the sauce.

13. Prepare the meat for cooking, as required.

14. Brown the meat thoroughly in a heavy pan with fat or in a hot oven (Fig.Â 15.11).

15. Remove the meat from the pan (if required) and brown mirepoix in remaining fat.

16. Add flour to make a roux. Brown the roux.

17. Add stock to make a thickened sauce. Add seasonings and flavorings.

18. Return the meat to the pan. Cover and simmer in the oven or on the range until the meat is tender.

19. Adjust the sauce as necessary (strain, season, reduce, dilute, etc.).

20. Evaluate the finished product (p. 449).
