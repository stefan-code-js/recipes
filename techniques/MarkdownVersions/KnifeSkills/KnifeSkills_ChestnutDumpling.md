## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Chestnut Dumpling 
Chestnuts and water are blended and the liquid is strained

2. This 
chestnut milk is reduced for up to two days until thick, then further 
reduced until it begins to caramelize and thicken, at which point the 
mixture is beaten in a mortar and pestle to smooth and aerate the 
chestnut mochi

3. The chestnut mochi is rolled thin in a pasta machine 
with cep oil and punched into round wrappers

4. Walnuts are blanched, pureed, and strained, and the walnut milk is 
then cooked down to a paste

5. The walnut paste is folded with whipped 
cream and set with gelatin, then aerated and frozen to create a walnut 
sponge

6. The sponge is sliced and cut into half-rounds

7. Chickens and ducks are simmered to make a stock, which is strained 
and left to settle

8. The fat is skimmed off the surface of the stock and 
reserved

9. The stock is reduced to a light glaze

10. The glaze is then mixed with some of the reserved fat, brought to 
a boil, and left to cool until a skin forms on top
