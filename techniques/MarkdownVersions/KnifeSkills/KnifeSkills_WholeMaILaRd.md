## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Whole Ma I la rd 
Wild mallards are plucked, leaving the feathers on the head and wings, 
then aged for five days

2. The ducks are lightly cured with koji cure, then 
grilled over charcoal, with birch bark creating smoke

3. As the duck is grilling, it's brushed with a glaze made by reducing 
duck stock, ryeso water, and mushroom-kelp broth

4. The remaining 
glaze is further reduced to make a finishing glaze

5. The breasts are carved from the duck and sliced, and duck marinade 
is brushed in between the slices, which are reassembled, repositioned 
on the duck, and brushed with finishing glaze
