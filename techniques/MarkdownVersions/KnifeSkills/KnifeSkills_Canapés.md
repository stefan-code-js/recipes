1. Good mise en place is essential.

2. Assemble as close as possible to serving time.

5. Use high-quality ingredients. Canapés can be a good way to utilize leftovers, but only if the leftovers have been carefully handled and stored to retain freshness.6. Keep it simple. Simple, neat arrangements are more attractive than elabo-rate, overworked designs. Besides, you don’t have time to get too fancy. Be sure the canapés hold together and do not fall apart in the customers’ hands.

6. Arrange the canapés carefully and attractively on trays. Much of the attraction of canapés is eye appeal, and the cus-tomer never sees just one at a time, but a full tray. Each tray should carry an assortment of flavors and textures, so there is something for every taste.
