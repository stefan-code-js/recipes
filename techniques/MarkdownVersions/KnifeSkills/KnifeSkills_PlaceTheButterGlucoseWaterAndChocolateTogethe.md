## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. 25 g pectin  
10 g bilberry powder  
Place the butter, glucose, water,  and chocolate together in a pot

2. Bring the mixture up to a boil 
and then add the remaining ingredients

3. Bring everything back up to a boil, whisking it to 
incorporate

4. Remove from the heat and  roll one -third of the mixture at a time between two 
large sheets of parchment

5. Roll it very thin with a rolling pin then allow it to cool

6. Use a small 
mussel shell to trace its’ shape all over the top sheet of parchment paper using a sharpie

7. From
there, cut the flat mussel shape out all the way through the crisp base and parchment underneath

8. Transfer the shapes, still sandwiched between the papers, to th e fridge to set

9. Once
cool, transfer to a baking sheet and place it into a 160°C (30% fan) oven to bake for 7 minutes.Remove the tray from the oven and allow it to cool down, then peel off the sheets of parchment

10. Return the tray to the oven for another 4  minutes
