## Overview

Potatoes Cooked in Soil 
Maitake and oyster mushroom "soils" are made by pureeing the 
mushrooms separately with oil, slowly cooking the purees until 
caramelized, straining off all the oil, and drying the resulting pulp. 
The maitake soil and oyster mushroom soil are mixed together and 
seasoned with roasted kelp salt. 
Roasted potatoes are passed through a sieve, then beaten with

A ramekin is placed into a terra-cotta herb pot

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Potatoes Cooked in Soil 
Maitake and oyster mushroom "soils" are made by pureeing the 
mushrooms separately with oil, slowly cooking the purees until 
caramelized, straining off all the oil, and drying the resulting pulp

2. The maitake soil and oyster mushroom soil are mixed together and 
seasoned with roasted kelp salt

3. Roasted potatoes are passed through a sieve, then beaten with 
potato stock, truffle juice, and butter to yield a smooth puree

4. A ramekin is placed into a terra-cotta herb pot and both are 
heated

5. Small new potatoes are boiled in a potato stock infused with 
fresh elderflower

6. One of the boiled new potatoes is positioned in the 
center of the ramekin; the others are removed from the potato stock 
and warmed in elderflower emulsion, then added to the ramekin

7. All 
the new potatoes are dotted with elderflower fudge, sprinkled with 
elderflower blossoms, and seasoned with elderflower oil

8. The potato 
puree is spread over everything

9. The mushroom soil is distributed over 
the top and a nasturtium vine is inserted into the center new potato
