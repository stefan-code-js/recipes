2. It is widely used in Indian cooking and its preservative properties make it a fine pickling spice for fruit and vegetables

3. BLENDING SCIENCE
Look to the secondary flavour compounds of nigella for effective spice pairings: 
significant concentrations of cymene give the spice its earthy, fresh aroma, and 
smaller amounts of the mild terpenes pinene and limonene are also present

4. Grains and pulses Flavour rice and 
bulgur wheat with lightly toasted seeds; fry briefly in oil or ghee and stir into  
just cooked dhal

5. Eggs Sprinkle over scrambled  
or fried eggs

6. Breads Combine with white sesame 
seeds and stir into flatbread dough or sprinkle on top

7. Also works well with  
rye breads

8. Goat’s cheese Work the seeds into 
a cheese dip made with whipped feta or other goat’s cheese

9. Lamb Use in a slow-braised Indian 
korma or Moroccan tagine
