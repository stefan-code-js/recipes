COOKING eGGS
Simmering In The Shell
the term hard-boiled egg  is not a good one to use because eggs should be simmered instead 
of boiled.
eggs may be simmered in water to the soft-, medium-, or hard-cooked stage according to the 
following methods.
Standards of Quality for Eggs Simmered in the Shell
1. Cooked to the proper degree of hardness or softness. the yolk should be still liquid for  
soft-cooked eggs, soft but no longer liquid for medium-cooked eggs

7. Drain immediately and cool under cold running water to stop the cooking. Cool just a few seconds if eggs are to be served hot. Cool further if they are to be held for later use.

8. To peel, crack the shell and pull it away, starting at the large end (where the air sac is located). For easier peeling, peel while still warm, and hold under running water to help loosen shell. Very fresh eggs are hard to peel. Eggs for cooking in the shell should be sev-eral days old.
