## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Place an empty volume measure on the left side of a balance scale. Balance the scale to zero.

2. Set the scale for the desired weight.

3. Pour batter into the measure until the scale balances.4. Note the volume of batter in the measure.

4. Pour the measured batter into a prepared pan, quickly scraping out the measure to get all the batter.

5. Scale the remaining cakes with the volume measure, using the volume noted in step 4.

6. Give the pans several sharp raps on the bench to free large trapped air bubbles. Bake immediately.

7. Have all prepared pans lined up on the bench.

8. Scale the first pan as for creamed batters.3. Quickly fill remaining pans to the same level as the first pan, judging the level by eye.

9. Spread the batter smooth and bake immediately.

10. Place the prepared cake pan on the left side of a balance scale. Balance the scale by placing another pan on the right side.

11. Set the scale for the desired weight.

12. Add batter to the left pan until the scale balances.

13. Remove the pan from the scale and spread the batter smooth with a spatula.

14. Repeat with remaining pans.

15. Give the pans several sharp raps on the bench to free large trapped air bubbles. Bake immediately.
