## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. 57PUFF PASTRIES
QUALITY BUTTER
To make puff pastry successfully, it is essential to use the finest butter, preferably unsalted and with a high 
butterfat content, ideally 84%  as it contains less water

2. In French this is called beurre de tourage and is 
malleable and softens more quickly

3. However, away from professional kitchens it can be difficult to buy so use butter with the highest fat content you can find

4. TEMPERATURE CONTROL
Work in a cool environment and make sure your equipment and your hands are cold, as the butter will be easier to handle

5. Using a marble slab also helps, particularly for making inverse puff pastry

6. THE SECRETS OF PUFF PASTRY: FOLDING AND TURNING
Creating the leaves in puff pastry is done by folding the dough over itself (making what are known as “turns”) and rotating the dough 45 degrees each time

7. Puff pastry is defined by the number of times it is 
rolled and folded and this can be four, five, or six

8. A single turn involves folding the dough in three, while a double turn requires four folds

9. The greater the number of turns, the lighter and crisper the pastry will be due to the greater number of leaves—a total that can reach 1 ,024

10. A SWISH OF THE BRUSH AND A FLOURISH OF THE ROLLING PIN
Between each turn, it is important to remove the dusting of flour on the work surface used to roll out the dough
