12. If eggs are not very fresh, add 1 teaspoon salt and 1 table-spoon distilled vinegar per quart of water (5 mL salt and 15 mL vinegar per L). The vinegar helps coagulate the egg white faster so it keeps a better shape.

13. Bring water to a simmer.

14. Break eggs, one at a time, into a dish or a small plate and slide into the simmering water. Eggs will hold their shape better if they slide in against the edge of the pan.

15. Simmer 3–5 minutes, until whites are coagulated but yolks are still soft.

16. Remove eggs from pan with slotted spoon or skimmer.
