## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Plum and Bee Larvae 
Sunflower seeds are cooked in brown butter and then ground with 
spices, reductions, roasted kelp salt, and Nordic shoyu to make a 
smooth paste

2. This paste is combined with freeze-dried rhubarb, 
lingonberries, and gooseberries soaked in dashi and seasoned with 
mushroom spice mix, all cooked down and caramelized in koji oil

3. The 
caramelized lingonberry-su nflower seed mixture is ground with fried 
parsley and fried lovage to make a paste

4. Plums are peeled and pitted but left whole, then dried until slightly 
chewy

5. Bee larvae are gently cooked in butter until the soft larvae firm 
up and then become crisp

6. Lingonber ry paste, Japanese quince paste, and seasoned parsley 
puree are piped into the cavity of the plum

7. Lemon thyme, coriander 
seeds, and morita chile also flavor the interior of the plum

8. The filled 
plum is then sliced

9. A teaspoon of elderflower-pollen oil is poured into a serving bowl 
and seasoned with cherry vinegar

10. Two slices of the filled plum are 
placed in the bowl and then brushed with more elderflower-pollen oil, 
dotted with Japanese quince paste, and topped with crispy bee larvae
