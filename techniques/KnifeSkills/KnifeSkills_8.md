# Substitutions Techniques: Maltodextrine et Alginate #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Molecular #Substitutions

**Preface:** A reference guide for disciplined ingredient substitution, maintaining the intended molecular structure when primary agents are unavailable.

**Ingredients**

- **Maltodextrin Function:** Absorbs fats, creates fine powder.

- **Sodium Alginate Function:** Spherification, requires calcium bath.

- **Substitutes (Maltodextrin):** Cornstarch (savory), powdered sugar (sweet).

- **Substitutes (Alginate):** Agar-agar (for manual spheres/gels).

**Method**

1. **Oil Powder Substitution:** When maltodextrin is unavailable, utilize cornstarch or powdered sugar as the carrier. Note that the resulting powder will possess a coarser texture. Alternatively, pivot the preparation to an oil gel.

2. **Spherification Substitution:** True liquid-to-liquid spherification is exclusive to the alginate-calcium reaction. When alginate is unavailable, simulate pearls by creating a firm agar gel and cutting it into precise miniature spheres.

**Technical Notes:** The substitution of agar for alginate fundamentally changes the spherification mechanism from chemical reaction to thermal setting.\
