3. Sauté the shallot in the butter just until it starts to become tender.

4. Optional step: Chop the lobster coral and liver (tomalley) and add to  the pan. Sauté just until firm, 10–20 seconds.

5. add the bread crumbs and brown them lightly in the butter. remove  from the heat.

6. add the parsley. Season the crumbs with salt and pepper.

8. Brush the tail well with melted butter.

9. place a few of the legs on top of the stuffing. If the lobster was split

10. place the lobster under the broiler at least 6 in. (15 cm) from the

11. at this point, the lobster will probably not be completely cooked,  unless it is very small and the broiler heat very low. place the pan  with the lobster in a hot oven to finish cooking.

12. remove the lobster from the heat and serve immediately with a  small cup of melted butter and with lemon garnish.

13. peel, devein, and butterfly the shrimp as shown in Figure 20.16.

14. place shrimp in individual service casserole dishes or in a shallow baking pan, tails up and cut side down. (Shrimp will curl more when cooked, so tails stand up as shrimp are broiled.)

15. Keep refrigerated until needed.

16. heat butter and oil in a saucepan until the butter is melted.

17. add the garlic, lemon juice, parsley, salt, and pepper.

18. pour the butter sauce over the shrimp.

19. place under the broiler at medium heat. Broil until the tops are lightly browned. (Don’t worry if the tips of the tails burn a little; this is normal.)

20. transfer the shrimp to the oven above the broiler for a few  minutes to finish cooking.
