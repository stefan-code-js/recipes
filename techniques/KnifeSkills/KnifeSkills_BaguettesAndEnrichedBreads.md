## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. BAGUETTES AND ENRICHED BREADS
129The poolish and the leaven are ready when they pass the float test

2. Drop 
a small amount of the poolish and the leaven into water

3. If either sinks, it is not 
ready to use and needs more time to ferment

4. To mix the baguette dough, measure the 400 grams of leaven and set 
aside the remainder to refresh and maintain as your starter

5. Pour the warm water 
into a large mixing bowl

6. Add the poolish and the leaven and stir to disperse

7. Add 
the all-purpose flour and the bread flour

8. Using your hands, mix thoroughly until 
no bits of dry flour remain

9. The baguette dough initially will feel a little stiffer 
than the basic country dough, but it will soften during bulk fermentation

10. Let the 
dough rest for 25 to 40 minutes
