## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. The item cooks in its own juices and, usually, a small amount of added liquid.

2. The item is served with its flavorful cooking liquid.

3. The product is cooked for a few minutes in fat over low heat, along with mirepoix or onion, to begin extracting juices. A little liquid is then added, the pot is covered, and the item is cooked.

4. The product is sautéed over high heat. Then other ingredients and liquids are added and the item is cooked, covered, over low heat. Example: Lobster à l’Americaine (p. 654).

5. The product is simply placed in a pot with liquids and flavoring ingredients. The pot is covered, and the item is steamed or simmered. Example: Moules Marinière (p. 655).
