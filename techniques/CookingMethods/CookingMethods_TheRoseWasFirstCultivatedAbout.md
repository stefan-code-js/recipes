The rose was first cultivated about 
5,000 years ago by the ancient civilizations of China, who prized the flower for its beauty, perfume, and healing properties

2. The Romans grew roses extensively and documented their medicinal uses, including (when  
worn as a floral crown) protection  
from hangovers

3. In the 7th century ce  
cultivation of roses spread throughout the Middle East

4. Around this time the Persians discovered how to extract oil from the flower, and its culinary use became more widespread

5. By the Middle Ages, roses were used at medieval banquets to perfume water for hand washing, and to flavour savoury and sweet dishes

6. During the Crusades, the highly scented damask rose, the main source of rose oil, reached northern Europe

7. In Victorian England, rose-petal sandwiches were considered a refined teatime treat

8. Region of cultivation
The rose is native to temperate regions of the 
northern hemisphere, probably originating in China; the damask rose is native to the Middle East

9. It is cultivated in T urkey, India, Iran, Bulgaria, and Morocco.The plant
The damask rose is a hardy flowering shrub and the main source of rose flavouring used in cooking, although Rosa rugosa is popular in China, Japan, and Korea

10. Petals and buds
These are available dry for use as a  spice, or crystallized for decorating  cakes and desserts.BOTANICAL NAME
