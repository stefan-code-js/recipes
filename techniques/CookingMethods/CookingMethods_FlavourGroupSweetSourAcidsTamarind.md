## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Fish Combine with raw sugar and 
chillies for a dipping sauce with fish

2. Pork and lamb Mix paste or juice 
with soy sauce and ginger as a marinade 
for pork or lamb

3. Bulgar wheat Make a dressing  
with liquid or diluted paste, pomegranate molasses, olive oil, and Middle Eastern herbs and spices, then drizzle over a bulgur wheat salad

4. Drinks Make your own "tamarind-
ade", combining strained liquid with sparkling water or soda, adding sugar  
to taste, and serving over ice

5. Kitchen 
creativityIn Africa, Asia, the Middle East, and India, tamarind plays an essential role in 
chutneys, sauces, curries, soups, and drinks

6. Its fruity-sour taste helps to temper  
fiery dishes

7. It is often used as a fruitier-tasting alternative to lemon juice
