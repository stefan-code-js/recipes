# Tartelette de Moutarde et Pomme de Terre #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Tart #Advanced

**Preface:** A technically advanced savoury tart, utilizing Dijon mustard in the shell to provide a disciplined, pungent base for a refined potato filling.Difficulty: Advanced

**Ingredients**

- **Mustard Tartelette Shells:**

  - 160 g all-purpose flour

  - 30 g Dijon mustard

  - 30 g cold water

  - 20 g Ligurian olive oil

  - 15 g egg yolk

  - 10 g granulated sugar

  - 3 g fine salt

- **Potato Filling:**

  - 300 g potato purée (smooth)

  - 100 g fresh cream

  - 50 g unsalted butter

  - 10 g fine salt

  - 5 g white pepper

  - 1 g nutmeg (freshly grated)

- **Garnish:**

  - Potato slices (confit)

  - Chives (finely chopped)

  - Mustard seeds (toasted)

**Method**

1. **Shells:** Combine flour, sugar, and salt. Form a well. Introduce the egg yolk, mustard, water, and olive oil. Mix and knead until a firm, cohesive dough forms. Rest in the refrigerator for a minimum of 30 minutes.

2. Roll the dough thinly. Line the tartelette molds. Blind bake at 170°C until a pale gold colour is achieved.

3. **Filling:** Combine the potato purée, cream, butter, salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Whip until the purée is light and aerated.

4. **Assembly:** Pipe the potato purée into the cooled shells using a disciplined technique.

5. **Finish:** Garnish the surface with confit potato slices, toasted mustard seeds, and a disciplined scattering of chopped chives.
